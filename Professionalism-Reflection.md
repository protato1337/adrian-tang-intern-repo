## Research & Learn

### What are examples of professional vs. unprofessional behaviour in a casual work environment? (Hint: Think about communication, meeting etiquette, and teamwork.)

### *Professional:*
* *Communicating clearly and politely.*
* *Being on time and prepared for meetings.*
* *Listening actively and not interrupting.*
* *Sharing credit and supporting teammates.*
* *Keeping discussions constructive, not personal.*
* *Using an appropriate tone in messages.*

### *Unprofessional:*
* *Ignoring messages or giving unclear one-word replies.*
* *Being late or unprepared.*
* *Gossiping or blaming others.*
* *Interrupting or dismissing ideas.*
* *Using rude, defensive, or hostile tone.*
* *Not respecting boundaries.*

### What does respectful communication look like in remote teams? (e.g., tone in messages, handling disagreements, responding to feedback)

* *Use a friendly, neutral tone.*
* *Avoid sarcasm or ambiguous jokes.*
* *Ask questions instead of making accusations.*
* *Acknowledge messages promptly.*
* *Handle disagreements with calm, factual discussion.*
* *Respond to feedback with openness.*

### How can you give and receive feedback professionally? (e.g., focus on actions, not people; assume good intent; ask for clarification instead of reacting defensively)

### *Giving feedback*
* *Focus on behaviour, not personality.*
* *Be specific and timely.*
* *Assume positive intent.*

### *Receiving feedback*
* *Listen fully before reacting.*
* *Ask clarifying questions.*
* *Avoid taking it personally.*
* *Thank the person and outline next steps.*

## Reflection

### Have you ever experienced or witnessed great teamwork and professionalism? What made it effective?

* *Clear roles, open communication, and shared goals.*
* *Team members support each other.*
* *Respectful discussion of different ideas.*
* *Everyone feels heard and valued.*

### What steps can you take to make sure your communication is clear, respectful, and inclusive?

* *Re-read messages for clarity and tone.*
* *Provide context in chats/emails.*
* *Avoid jargon or assumptions.*
* *Use inclusive language.*
* *Consider differences in communication styles.*
* *Ask if your explanation makes sense.*

### How can you help create a positive, fun, and professional work environment?

* *Be friendly and approachable.*
* *Celebrate wins and acknowledge contributions.*
* *Keep humour light and inclusive.*
* *Offer help when teammates are stuck.*
* *Avoid negativity and drama.*
* *Show appreciation regularly.*

### How can you prepare effectively for meetings with supervisors and colleagues?

* *Review the agenda beforehand.*
* *Bring necessary documents or updates.*
* *Prepare questions or talking points.*
* *Join a few minutes early.*
* *Take notes and summarise action items.*

### What would it look like to be proactive during your internship?

* *Ask for tasks when free.*
* *Suggest improvements politely.*
* *Keep track of completed and pending work.*
* *Anticipate needs and prepare early.*
* *Follow up on tasks without needing reminders.*

### How might you follow up with colleagues or supervisors if you need information/action from them? How would you escalate it further if you don't get a response and when would you escalate?

### *Following up*
* *Send a polite reminder after a reasonable time (e.g., 2–3 days).*
* *Provide context so they don’t need to search for the details.*

### *Escalating*
* *Only escalate after multiple reminders with no response.*
* *Share facts, not blame, when escalating.*
* *Escalate when delays affect deadlines or team goals.*

### What are some things you won't do?

* *Gossip or complain unnecessarily.*
* *Ignore messages or tasks.*
* *Take feedback personally or react emotionally.*
* *Overstep boundaries or bypass supervisors too early.*
* *Blame others instead of taking responsibility.*
* *Make assumptions without clarifying.*
